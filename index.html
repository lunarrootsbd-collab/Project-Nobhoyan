About the Project

Project NOBHOYAN is an independent, student-led initiative that began with a simple motivation: to make science learning more interactive, exploratory, and inspiring. In traditional classrooms, physics and astronomy are often taught through rote memorization of formulas and problem drills. We wanted to change that. Instead of copying equations from the board, what if students could stand on distant exoplanets, look up, and experience the universe firsthand?

Out of this vision grew an interactive learning platform for astronomical objects‚Äîa web-based simulator that renders the night sky as it would appear from over 5,700 confirmed exoplanets. Using data from NASA‚Äôs Exoplanet Archive and the GAIA catalog of more than one billion stars, our platform faithfully reproduces stellar positions, colors, magnitudes, and motions. Every star is plotted with real scientific data, giving students not just a picture of the cosmos, but a dataset they can investigate.

On our platform, learners can search and select exoplanets, rotate through a 3D field of stars, and then step into immersive views of the night sky. They can draw and label custom constellations, explore a ‚ÄúResearch Lab‚Äù that enables plotting astrophysical quantities such as brightness versus temperature, and click on individual stars to reveal their true properties‚Äîradius, temperature, metallicity, distance, and more. Colors are rendered authentically, ensuring that the sky is not only beautiful but also scientifically accurate.

Behind the scenes, our six-member team collaborated to bring this vision to life. From data collection and JSON preprocessing to UI/UX design and front-end development, we each contributed skills that made the project possible. Weekly sprints, code reviews, and design sessions kept us on track, while outreach efforts extended our impact beyond the screen. We conducted workshops in Dhaka, Rajshahi, and Khulna, introducing over 500 students and dozens of teachers to the simulator. For many, it was their first time questioning why stars differ in color or how the universe might look from worlds beyond Earth.

Today, Project NOBHOYAN continues as both a teaching tool and a community effort. It‚Äôs more than a sky simulator‚Äîit‚Äôs a step toward reimagining how science education can spark curiosity, foster discovery, and make the vast universe accessible to everyone.

Explore Our Web App

Hopping Page

The Hopping page presents a 3D point cloud of 4,302 ExoHosts‚Äîstars that host exoplanets‚Äîusing data from NASA‚Äôs Exoplanet Archive. These stars are plotted in 3D space based on their right ascension, declination, and parallax. Star sizes are determined by their radius, while colors are accurately mapped using temperature and BV index, ensuring a realistic and scientifically accurate visualization. Users can freely navigate through this stellar landscape, search for stars, and view informative pop-ups.

Exoplanets are also rendered with custom textures based on radius, eccentricity, and semimajor axis, providing immersive detail. Clicking them reveals detailed scientific information.

Exosky Page

By double-clicking any exohost, users are seamlessly transported to the Planetarium page, where they can observe the night sky from the surface of that star's exoplanets. Utilizing data from the Gaia Catalogue and custom algorithms, we ensure that star positions, sizes, and brightness are scientifically accurate. Users can zoom, adjust planet pole orientation, and access advanced tools for exploration.

  • Draw Constellation: Create and name custom constellations.
  • Drag & Research: Generate HR Diagrams from selected regions.
